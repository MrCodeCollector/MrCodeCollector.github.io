ABOUT

What is Hack the Bay?



Hack the Bay is the Bay Area's annual hackathon run by students from Abraham Lincoln High School.

We want to encourage students from all backgrounds to come together, collaborate, and bring to life real world applications that create positive change. Our goal is to not only generate positive change in the community, but also to create an environment that brings all the necessary tools for creativity, networking and development in one place, providing several students with the opportunity to create an impact.

FAQ

When and where is Hack the Bay? Hack the Bay will be on Saturday - Sunday, May 21- 22, at Make School's San Francisco location.

Who can participate?
 Any high school student can participate in this event. If you've already graduated from high school, you can't participate. However, you may sign up to be a mentor!

Does this cost money?
 Nope! Hack the Bay is completely free for all attendees. Oh, and did I mention there's also FREE FOOD AND SWAG?

What should I bring?
 You should bring your laptop & charger (we won't provide computers), school ID, phone & charger, and power strips (recommended). We'll be providing food!

I don't have a team/idea, what now?
 You can form teams prior, or the day of the event, where we will be hosting a session for you to meet others and share ideas.

How many hackers per team?
 Team sizes will have a maximum of 4 members.

What types of hardware can I hack on?
 We will have a list of hardware you will be able to hack on as we get closer to the date of the event.
